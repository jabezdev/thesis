# LGU Dashboard Usability Survey (Short Version)

## 1) Purpose
This short survey is designed for LGU users to evaluate the usability of the Panahon dashboard as a website-based decision support tool.

Focus areas:
- Website/dashboard navigation and clarity
- Readability and interpretability of charts/tables
- Operational usefulness for alerts and decisions
- Overall perceived usability in a concise form

Target completion time: 5-7 minutes.

## Project Introduction
Panahon is a web-based monitoring and decision-support platform developed to help LGU teams monitor weather conditions, review historical records, and act on alerts.
This study is part of the project evaluation phase and aims to measure whether the dashboard is practical, understandable, and usable in real LGU workflows.

## Section B. Website and Dashboard Usability
Instruction: For Q4-Q11, use this scale.
1 = Strongly disagree
2 = Disagree
3 = Neutral
4 = Agree
5 = Strongly agree

4. Navigation is consistent and predictable across dashboard pages.
5. Labels and terms are clear for LGU work (no confusing jargon).
6. I can quickly find the information I need.
7. Key metrics are visible at a glance on the dashboard.
8. Charts and tables are easy to read and interpret.
9. Alert information is clear enough for action/coordination.
10. The dashboard works well on my usual device and screen size.
11. The interface is clean and not cluttered.

## 4) Scoring and Analysis

## Quick scoring
- Section B score: mean of Q4-Q11
- UMUX-Lite short score: mean of Q12 and Q13
- Overall score: Q14 as a single global rating

Interpretation guide (practical):
- 4.2-5.0: strong usability
- 3.4-4.1: acceptable, with improvement areas
- below 3.4: significant usability concerns

## Suggested analysis outputs
- Report means/medians for Q4-Q13 and Q14.
- Compare by role and frequency of use.
- Thematically code Q15-Q16 to identify recurring pain points.
